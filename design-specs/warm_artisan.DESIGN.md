## Overview

Use warmth as material. The page should feel crafted, tactile, and intimate, with natural pacing and real narrative weight.

## Colors

Favor cream, clay, muted earth, and soft olive. Surfaces should feel like paper, ceramic, linen, or wood rather than polished software panels.

## Typography

Display typography should feel literary and inviting. Body copy should stay human, warm, and readable with generous line height.

## Layout

Use breathing room, framed process imagery, and narrative-led sections. Let the page feel made, not assembled.

## Elevation & Depth

Depth should stay low-contrast and warm. Prefer layered paper-like surfaces and soft framing over sharp modern shadows.

## Shapes

Rounding is generous and calm. It should reinforce hospitality and hand-crafted confidence.

## Components

Buttons should feel inviting and substantial. Story and gallery panels should behave like tactile surfaces, not generic content cards.

## Do's and Don'ts

- Do preserve warmth and human pacing.
- Do make process and materiality visible.
- Don't shift into cold corporate minimalism.
- Don't use neon or icy accents.
